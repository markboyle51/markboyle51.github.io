My Dad

Mark Boyle

Mark was a very loving family man. Nothing meant more to him than his children and grandchildren. He was a hardworking man who knew how to have fun. A good time was always to be had with Mark. He was the kind of guy who could light up a room.


Father

(Greatest Dad Ever)

Mark was an amazing family man. His children could always count on him to be there, especially when it matter. He never missed an opportunity to lift his kids up. Nobody believed in his children more than Mark did. He saw something special in each on of his kids and spent a lifetime encouraging them to let it shine.

Grandpa

(Papa Mark)

Mark spent all of his free time with his grandkids. He would show up in the middle of the day just to watch cartoons with them. Even though Mark had bad knees and a hard time walking he would never say no when his grandkids wanted to go for a bike ride or play in the backyard. Marks favorite past time was just being with his grandkids, no matter what they had planned whether it was a nice lunch and some shopping or just hanging out at home.

Business Man

(Cement Contractor)

Mark was a hardworking Concrete contractor. He was the first local contractor to have a curb and gutter machine in the valley and did all the curb and gutter west of the 99. One of his first big projects was constructing the Ashlan overpass, which still stands today. While he was never formally educated as an engineer he, all by himself, engineered some of the most complicated cement projects on the west coast, including a cantilever deck over the San Francisco Bay meant to hold some of the biggest cranes in the United States.

Best Friend

(Ex Son-in-Law)

Mark had a very special relationship with a very unexpected person. His best friend was his ex father-in-law Frank Caglia. On the weekends, the two were inseparable. Mark, along with his kids, and Frank were always fixing one of Frank's many ragtag projects. One day they might be fixing frozen water pipes at the Warnors Theater and the next maybe saving some old Fresno landmark from destruction. Mark, with his kids, spent every Tuesday night Barbecuing steaks for his ex-in-laws and every sunday morning making them breakfast.

Dog Owner

(Animal Lover)

Mark had two beautiful dogs named Lucy and Barbie. These two special ladies went everywhere Mark did. That included all of his errands, like the store and even the Doctors office. If it was too hot or cold outside Mark would leave his car running just to make sure his girls stayed comfortable. He would hand make all of their meals and take them for daily rides to his daughter's house to play with his grandkids and maybe go for a swim.

  • Early Life

    Mark was born in Santa Cruz, Ca. He was the biggest baby ever born in Santa Cruz county at the time. He was the youngest of four children. His family later moved to Fresno where he grew up and went to Bullard High School. During highschool Mark played football and was a caretaker for an elderly disabled man. During his Junior year he met his first and only true love Bernadette Caglia.

    Young Mark

  • Family Life

    Mark married his highschool sweetheart Bernadette. Together they had three children, Nicole, Danielle, and Frankie. Even though Mark and Bernadette divorced in 1981 he loved her to the day he died. Mark spent much of his life and all of his free time doting on his family

    Mark and his Family

  • Retirement

    Mark spent most of his free time taking of his dogs and spending time at his daughter's house with his family. When Mark was home he would spend hours watching Documentaries on anything history related. Mark was fascinated with history his whole life and finally had time to learn about all the things that interested him.
